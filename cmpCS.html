Fidelity Investments

Electronic Funds Transfers

The Project

I worked on this project as part of a week long sprint. I worked with a project manager, a business analyst and a whole team of ux designers helping to create a better EFT (Electronic Funds Transfer) flow.

Problem

Electronic Funds Transfers (or EFTs) allow people to easily move money from one account to another. Many customers trying to transfer money digitaly, ended up either mailing in forms or calling Fidelity call centers for help. I was tasked with helping customers complete the digital process from start to finish.

What the user is prompted to download, fill out and mail in if they accidentally choose the wrong option.

PROJECT ONE

Many users would select the wrong options for their situation and the website would prompt them to fill out an incredibly long form (below). What the users didn't know was that they didn't always need to be filling out the form. In many cases it was just a back up if they failed in EFT flow.

Design Projects

EFT User Flow

THE PROBLEM

Customers who own businesses in their own name do not actually own the account, the business does (it just happens to be in their name). Within the previous path, business oweners continually clicked "Yes, I own this account" and would end up failing futhur down the flow.

Design Projects

Solutions

I created six different possibilites for how the screens could potentially look. The team decided that the best solution was a "Yes" or "No" tab. This allowed the user to pick "Yes" (thinking they own it, when in reality they don't) and then select "My business". This would take them to the same place as clicking "No" in the tab option.
